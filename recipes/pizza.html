Pizza

A cheese pizza is a classic pizza that is simple yet delicious. It typically consists of a round, flat pizza crust that is topped with tomato sauce and a generous amount of melted cheese. The most common type of cheese used is mozzarella, which has a mild flavor and a smooth, creamy texture when melted. The cheese is usually evenly spread over the pizza, covering the sauce and extending all the way to the crust.

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough
  • Tomato sauce or pizza sauce
  • Cheese (mozzarella is the most common choice, but you can use other types like provolone or fontina as well)

    • Optional

  • Olive oil
  • Garlic
  • Italian herbs (oregano, basil, thyme)
  • Red pepper flakes

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C).
  2. Roll out your pizza dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness and shape.
  3. Spread tomato sauce or pizza sauce evenly over the dough, leaving about a 1/2 inch border around the edges.
  4. Sprinkle your desired amount of cheese over the sauce, making sure to cover the sauce completely.
  5. Add any additional toppings you like, such as vegetables or meats.
  6. Optional: Drizzle olive oil over the top of the pizza and sprinkle garlic, Italian herbs, and/or red pepper flakes on top.
  7. Bake the pizza in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly, and the crust is golden brown.
  8. Remove from the oven and let the pizza cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.
